Advanced soroban is not only about faster hands or faster thought. It is about staying readable while the pace rises.

## Core idea

Speed becomes useful only when it preserves three things:

- the running total
- the sign of the next move
- a quick way to detect drift

## Worked example: 74 + 8 - 3 + 12

1. Start from <strong>74</strong>.
2. Add <strong>8</strong> to reach <strong>82</strong>.
3. Subtract <strong>3</strong> to reach <strong>79</strong>.
4. Add <strong>12</strong> to reach <strong>91</strong>.

This sequence is not hard because of the numbers. It is hard because the rhythm changes twice.

## Error-check rule

Use one short safety check after each move:

1. Does the new total feel larger or smaller in the right direction?
2. Did the last operation affect the correct place?
3. Can you still say the full total, not only the last digit?

## Common mistakes

- Going faster after a correct step and losing the next sign.
- Only hearing the last digit and forgetting the whole number.
- Continuing after a suspected drift instead of checking immediately.

## Practice habit

Use timed worksheets only after you can keep the running total stable in untimed sets. Speed should reveal structure, not destroy it.
