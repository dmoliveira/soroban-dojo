After the first division patterns, the next job is not "harder division" yet. It is stronger quotient recognition.

## Core idea

When the divisor is known, your task is to find the missing factor quickly and then prove it.

For early exact division:

1. Read the dividend clearly.
2. Ask what factor of the divisor fits exactly.
3. Name the quotient.
4. Reverse the operation to verify it.

## Worked example: 24 ÷ 6

1. Start from **24**.
2. Ask: **what times 6 gives 24?**
3. The answer is **4**.
4. Verify it at once: **4 × 6 = 24**.

## Worked example: 36 ÷ 9

1. Start from **36**.
2. Ask: **what times 9 gives 36?**
3. The quotient is **4**.
4. Check again: **4 × 9 = 36**.

Even though both examples end with `4`, the divisor changes the reasoning rhythm. That is why it helps to say the fact family fully.

## Quotient habit

Use this short script during practice:

- "Dividend is 24."
- "Divisor is 6."
- "Missing factor is 4."
- "Check: 4 times 6 is 24."

This keeps division from becoming a vague guess.

## Common mistakes

- Naming a quotient without a check.
- Mixing up dividend and divisor.
- Relying on the shape of the number instead of the factor relationship.

Before doing larger division later, become reliable with exact fact families. Exact division is the training ground for calm quotient placement.
